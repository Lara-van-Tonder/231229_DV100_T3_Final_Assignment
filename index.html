TRONDHEIM

Trondheim is a historic city located in central Norway. It is the third-largest city in Norway by population and serves as the administrative center of Trøndelag County. Trondheim has a wide variety of activities and experiences to explore. There is something for everyone to enjoy!

TROMSØ

Tromsø, located in the northern part of Norway, is a popular destination known for its stunning natural beauty and unique Arctic experiences. Experience the Northern Lights in all their glory in one of the best places in the world to view them.

LOFOTEN

The Lofoten Islands, often referred to simply as Lofoten, are an archipelago located off the northwest coast of Norway, within the Arctic Circle. Lofoten is known for its stunning natural beauty, dramatic landscapes, and vibrant fishing villages. This is a must visit location for thrill seekers.

GEIRANGERFJORD

Geirangerfjord is one of the most famous and stunning fjords in Norway, renowned for its awe-inspiring natural beauty and dramatic landscapes. In 2005, Geirangerfjord, along with the nearby Nærøyfjord, was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This recognition was due to its exceptional natural beauty and unique fjord landscape, making it a protected area.

BERGEN

Bergen is a vibrant and historic city located on the southwestern coast of Norway. It is the second-largest city in Norway after Oslo and is often referred to as the Gateway to the Fjords due to its proximity to some of the country's most famous fjords. Bergen is surrounded by natural beauty, including fjords, mountains, and the North Sea. The city itself is nestled between seven hills and features charming, colorful wooden houses along its waterfront, creating a picturesque setting.

SVOLVÆR

Svolvær is a charming coastal town located in the Lofoten archipelago in northern Norway. It is the largest town in the Lofoten Islands and serves as the administrative center of the Vågan Municipality. Svolvær serves as one of the best locations for whale watching enthusiasts to get up close and personal with the gentle giants of the sea.

Norway is a stunning and diverse country that offers a wide range of attractions and experiences, making it a compelling destination for travelers. Norway is renowned for its breathtaking natural landscapes, from majestic fjords like Geirangerfjord and Nærøyfjord to rugged mountain ranges, pristine lakes, and dramatic coastlines. The country's scenery is truly awe-inspiring and provides ample opportunities for outdoor activities and photography. Norway is one of the best places on Earth to witness the Northern Lights (Aurora Borealis). In the northern regions, particularly during the winter months, you can experience the magical display of colorful lights dancing across the Arctic skies. Norway's fjords are world-famous for their beauty and grandeur. Exploring these deep, narrow inlets by cruise, kayak, or hiking offers a unique perspective on the country's natural wonders. Norway is a playground for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you're into hiking, skiing, snowboarding, kayaking, fishing, or simply enjoying the great outdoors, Norway has something to offer for all levels of adventure seekers.

A Norwegian cruise offers a unique and scenic way to explore the stunning coastal landscapes, fjords, and cultural gems of Norway. Here's what you can typically expect on a Norwegian cruise:
  1. Fjord Exploration: One of the main highlights of a Norwegian cruise is the opportunity to explore Norway's famous fjords. Cruise itineraries often include visits to renowned fjords like the Geirangerfjord, Nærøyfjord, and Sognefjord, allowing you to marvel at their dramatic landscapes, towering cliffs, and cascading waterfalls.
  2. Picturesque Ports: Norwegian cruises typically stop at charming coastal towns and cities along the way. Ports of call may include Bergen, Stavanger, Ålesund, Tromsø, and others, each with its own unique culture, history, and attractions. You'll have the chance to explore these destinations, visit local landmarks, and sample Norwegian cuisine.
  3. Northern Lights and Midnight Sun: Depending on the time of year, you may have the opportunity to witness the Northern Lights (Aurora Borealis) during winter cruises, especially in the northern regions of Norway. In the summer, you can experience the Midnight Sun, with 24-hour daylight in the northern areas.
  4. Excursions and Activities: Cruise lines offer a variety of shore excursions and activities to enhance your experience. These can include guided tours of cultural sites, hiking and biking adventures, wildlife safaris, and even dog sledding and snowmobiling in winter.
  5. Onboard Amenities: Norwegian cruise ships are known for their modern amenities and comfortable accommodations. Depending on the cruise line and ship, you can expect features like fine dining restaurants, spa facilities, fitness centers, entertainment options, and relaxing lounges.

Safety is a top priority on cruise ships, and cruise lines implement a variety of measures to ensure the well-being of passengers and crew members. These measures are designed to address various safety concerns, including accidents, illnesses, and emergencies. While specific protocols may vary from cruise line to cruise line, here are some common safety measures taken on a cruise:
  1. Muster Drill: Before the ship sets sail, all passengers are required to participate in a muster drill. During this drill, passengers are instructed on safety procedures, such as how to don life jackets and where to assemble in case of an emergency. It is essential to attend the muster drill as it provides critical information on what to do in case of an emergency.
  2. Lifeboat Drills: Crew members regularly conduct lifeboat drills to ensure that all lifeboats and safety equipment are in working order. Passengers may also participate in these drills, depending on the cruise line's policies.
  3. Sanitation and Hygiene: Cruise ships have strict sanitation protocols to prevent the spread of illnesses, including gastrointestinal diseases. This includes regular cleaning and disinfection of common areas, as well as hand hygiene measures such as hand sanitizing stations throughout the ship.
  4. Medical Facilities: Cruise ships are equipped with medical facilities and trained medical staff to handle medical emergencies. Passengers with specific medical conditions or needs should inform the ship's medical team.
  5. Security Measures: Cruise ships have security personnel to ensure the safety and security of passengers and crew. Access to certain areas of the ship may be restricted, and there may be surveillance cameras in public areas.
  6. Emergency Response: Cruise ships have comprehensive emergency response plans in place, which cover various scenarios, including fires, collisions, or other emergencies. Crew members undergo rigorous training to respond to these situations efficiently.
  7. Safety Equipment: Cruise ships are equipped with modern safety equipment, including lifeboats, life rafts, life vests, fire suppression systems, and more. Regular maintenance and inspections are conducted to ensure their functionality.
  8. Guest Services: Passengers can typically contact guest services 24/7 for assistance or to report any concerns. They can also provide information on safety procedures and help with any medical or security issues.
  9. Communication: Cruise lines use various communication methods to relay important information to passengers, such as public address systems, in-cabin announcements, and informational brochures. In the event of an emergency, passengers will receive clear instructions on what to do.
  10. Training: Cruise ship crew members undergo extensive training in safety procedures and emergency response. They are well-prepared to handle a range of situations to ensure the safety of passengers and fellow crew members.
It's important for passengers to familiarize themselves with the ship's safety procedures and to follow the instructions provided by the cruise line and crew members. Passengers should also stay informed about any specific safety measures related to their cruise, as these can vary based on the cruise line, ship, and destination.
